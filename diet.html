Recommended Daily Diet

In the wild, Cockatiels feed at ground level, eating berries, fruits, grass seeds, grains and many other foods. Therefore, it's very important for them to have a varied diet in captivity for them to recieve the required intake of the same nutrients and vitamins as they would in the wild.
Their diet should consist of:

60 - 70% should be made up of pellets

  • Each store bought pellet will have measured and balanced nutrition so they get what they need
  • These are better than a seed heavy diet (as often provided by owners) as Cockatiels usually selectively pick their favourite seeds to eat and discard the vitamin rich seed hulls. Seeds also have a high fat content which isn't ideal for everyday meals.

10 - 30% should be made up of fresh vegetables, fruits and protein

  • They have high vitamin requirements so dark green vegetables and others with a variety of nutrients are benificial
  • Some reccomended for their high vitamin A content include bell peppers, carrots, sweet potatoes, tomatoes which are to be eaten in in small quantities
  • Others to try include: peas, broccoli, kale, spinach, carrot, sweet potato, a small amount of sprouted seeds, legumes, chick-weed and dandelion

5% should be snacks and treats

  • Snacks (like seeds and rewards) should be monitered to not make up too much of their diet due to high fat contents
